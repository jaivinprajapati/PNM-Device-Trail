PNM Device Trail

Open items & path forward  ·  as of Apr 2026

Objective
Wiom must know — at any point in time — the identity, current partner assignment, status, and full trail of every PNM device. PNM devices must never be installed on a customer. Partner-level accountability (custody, liability) must be defined and enforced in the system.
Fundamental questions
What is a PNM device?
A router placed at a partner's location solely to monitor internet health. It cannot be installed on a customer. Distinguished from regular devices (GX, SY series) by its device ID pattern (PNM001…).
Sorted
Is the partner liable for a PNM device, or is it solely Wiom's responsibility?
Assumption taken: Wiom holds full ownership and responsibility for PNM devices. The partner has no financial liability — no deposit is collected, and no loss process applies to the partner if a device goes missing. This assumption needs Dhruv's confirmation before being finalised.
Assumed
How is a PNM device identified in the system?
Currently by naming convention only (PNM001…) — convention, not a constraint. Decision needed: add an explicit flag in SSOT (e.g. device_type = 'PNM') so all system logic — installation guard, loss process, partner assignment — checks a reliable identifier, not a naming pattern.
Open
Which partner has which PNM device, and where does that mapping live?
Ground truth exists in Shariq's sheet (one device per partner) but is outside the system entirely. SSOT currently shows all PNM devices under the Wiom PNM partner — it does not reflect the actual partner holding the device.
Open
What states can a PNM device be in?
Not formally defined. Expected states: at warehouse → in transit → at partner office → returned. No system currently tracks these transitions or who triggered them.
Open
Can a PNM device be accidentally installed on a customer?
Customer team has put in some checks. We need to validate these are working and take ownership — ensuring the guard checks against the PNM identifier, not just the naming convention.
Checklist
How does Wiom monitor a PNM device's internet health?
Partially in place — NAS IDs are generated by Asif and enable monitoring via Remote T_Device. Akhil owns alerting. But SSOT does not know the NAS ID for any PNM device — currently updated manually (Jaivin shares IDs, Rahul updates SSOT). This manual step must be eliminated; NAS ID must flow into SSOT automatically at the point of generation.
Open
What happens when a PNM device is lost or damaged at a partner's location?
Based on the assumption that Wiom owns full responsibility — no partner consequence. Wiom absorbs the loss. No defined internal process either. Needs to be defined.
Open
How does a PNM device get returned to Wiom?
No defined process. Who initiates it, how is the handoff logged, and how does SSOT reflect the return — all undefined. Needs a clear state transition back to warehouse.
Open
